Cadences

A cadence is the ending of a phrase in music.

It is similar to a full stop, comma or an exclamation mark at the end of a sentence.

Ball
  • Perfect cadence

    • A perfect cadence ends from a chord V to a chord I.

  • Imperfect cadence

    • An imperfect cadence can either end from a chord I to a chord V, a chord II to a chord V, or a chord IV to a chord V.

  • Interrupted cadence

    • An interrupted cadence ends from a chord V to a chord VI. This changes the key from a major to a minor.

  • Plagal cadence

    • A plagal cadence ends from a chord IV to a chord I.
